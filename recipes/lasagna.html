Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that is beloved around the world. It consists of layers of wide, flat pasta sheets (lasagna noodles) interspersed with rich and savory fillings, typically a hearty meat sauce (ragù), creamy béchamel sauce, and various cheeses like mozzarella and Parmesan. The dish is baked until golden, bubbly, and tender, resulting in a comforting and satisfying meal. The combination of textures and flavors—the soft pasta, the rich sauce, the gooey cheese, and the crispy edges—makes it a quintessential comfort food.

Ingredients

For The Meat Sauce

  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 lean)
  • 1/2 lb ground Italian sausage
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped [1]
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes [1]
  • 1 (15-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried basil

For The Cheese Filling

  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp salt

For the Béchamel (White Sauce) & Assembly

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 9-12 lasagna noodles (oven-ready or regular, dried)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese for topping

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C)
    2.
  2. Prepare the Meat Sauce:
    3.
    • Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent (about 5-7 minutes)
    • Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute until fragrant.
    • Increase heat to medium-high. Add the ground beef and Italian sausage. Cook, breaking up the meat with a wooden spoon, until browned and cooked through. Drain excess grease if necessary.
    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low, cover, and let simmer for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld
  3. Prepare the Béchamel Sauce:
    4.
    • While the meat sauce simmers, in a separate medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for about 1 minute, stirring constantly.
    • Gradually whisk in the milk until the mixture is smooth. Continue cooking over medium heat, whisking frequently, until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon (about 5-8 minutes).
    • Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper, then remove from heat.
  4. Prepare the Cheese Mixture:
    5.
    • In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, beaten egg, 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, parsley (if using), and salt. Mix well until smooth
  5. Assemble the Lasagna:
    6.
    • Note: If using regular noodles (not oven-ready), boil them according to package directions and drain well before assembly.
    • Spread about 1/2 cup of the meat sauce across the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
    • Arrange a single layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
    • Spread half of the ricotta cheese mixture evenly over the noodles.
    • Top with about 1/4 of the remaining meat sauce.
    • Drizzle about 1/3 of the béchamel sauce over the meat sauce.
    • Sprinkle a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese.
    • Repeat the layers once more: noodles, remaining ricotta mixture, 1/4 of the meat sauce, 1/3 of the béchamel, and mozzarella.
    • Add a final layer of noodles, the remaining meat sauce, and the last of the béchamel sauce.
    • Top everything with the remaining mozzarella and the remaining 1/2 cup of Parmesan cheese
  6. Bake:
    7.
    • Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil (tent slightly so it doesn't touch the cheese).
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes
    • Remove the foil and bake for another 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbly
    • Let the lasagna rest for at least 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving. This helps it set and makes serving cleaner.
